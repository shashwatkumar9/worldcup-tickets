## Breaking the Quinto Partido Curse: Mexico's Recurring Nightmare

Seven consecutive World Cups. Seven consecutive eliminations in the Round of 16. Mexico's inability to reach the quarter-finals since 1986 (on home soil) has become the defining narrative of El Tri. They've fallen to the [USA (2002)](/teams/usa-world-cup-2026-tickets), [Argentina (2006, 2010)](/teams/argentina-world-cup-2026-tickets), [Netherlands (2014)](/teams/netherlands-world-cup-2026-tickets), [Brazil (2018)](/teams/brazil-world-cup-2026-tickets), and failed to escape the group in 2022.

But 2026 offers something previous tournaments couldn't: home advantage, altitude, and three venues filled with green-clad supporters singing "Cielito Lindo." If Mexico can top [Group A](/fixtures/group-a-world-cup-2026-tickets), a potentially favorable [Round of 32](/fixtures/round-of-32-world-cup-2026-tickets) draw awaits. A deep run could see El Tri playing [Quarter-Finals](/fixtures/quarter-finals-world-cup-2026-tickets) at venues like [SoFi Stadium in Los Angeles](/venues/sofi-stadium-world-cup-2026-tickets) or [Arrowhead Stadium in Kansas City](/venues/arrowhead-stadium-world-cup-2026-tickets), where large Mexican-American populations will create home-like atmospheres.
