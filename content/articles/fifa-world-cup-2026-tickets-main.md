## H2: Tournament Overview - The First 48-Team World Cup

The FIFA World Cup 2026 marks a revolutionary moment in football history as the tournament expands from 32 to 48 teams. This expansion creates 16 additional spots for nations to qualify, providing unprecedented opportunities for countries from every confederation to compete on football's biggest stage. The new format features 12 groups of four teams each, followed by a knockout stage beginning with the Round of 32.

### H3: Group Stage Structure

The 48 teams will be divided into 12 groups ([Group A World Cup 2026 Tickets](/fixtures/group-a-world-cup-2026-tickets) through [Group L World Cup 2026 Tickets](/fixtures/group-l-world-cup-2026-tickets)) with four teams in each group. ## H2: Travel Planning for FIFA World Cup 2026

### H3: Visa Requirements

**United States**
- ESTA (Electronic System for Travel Authorization) required for visa waiver countries
- Apply at least 72 hours before travel
- Valid for 2 years, multiple entries
- Cost: $21

**Mexico**
- FMM (Forma Migratoria Múltiple) tourist card required
- Free at border or airport
- Valid for 180 days
- Some nationalities require visa - check before booking

**Canada**
- ETA (Electronic Travel Authorization) required for visa-exempt countries
- Apply online, instant or within 72 hours
- Valid for 5 years
- Cost: CAD $7

### H3: Transportation Between Host Cities

**Air Travel**
- Major airports in all 16 host cities
- Domestic flights most efficient for long distances
- Book early for best prices (3-6 months advance)
- Consider multi-city tickets

**Ground Transportation**
- USA: Amtrak trains, Greyhound buses, rental cars
- Mexico: ADO buses (excellent service), domestic flights
- Canada: VIA Rail, excellent intercity bus service

**Local Transportation**
- Most venues accessible via public transit
- Stadium shuttles available on match days
- Uber/Lyft widely available in North American cities
- Consider staying near venue or public transit

### H3: Accommodation Strategies

**Book Early** (12+ months in advance)
- Hotels near venues fill quickly
- Prices increase dramatically closer to tournament
- Consider staying slightly outside city centers

**Alternative Accommodations**
- Airbnb/VRBO for groups and families

**Q: When do FIFA World Cup 2026 tickets go on sale?**

A: Prices range from $50 for Category 4 group-stage matches to $5,000+ for Category 1 Final tickets. Most group-stage matches range $100-$500 depending on category and teams playing.

**Q: Can I buy tickets for specific teams?**
A: FIFA offers team-specific packages allowing you to follow one team through the group stage (3 matches). Individual match tickets are also available but teams are confirmed only after the draw.

**Q: Where is the World Cup 2026 Final?**
A: The FIFA World Cup 2026 Final will be held at MetLife Stadium in East Rutherford, New Jersey (New York metro area) on July 19, 2026.

**Q: Do I need visas for all three host countries?**
A: Not if you're only attending matches in one country, but you'll need appropriate travel authorizations (ESTA for USA, FMM for Mexico, ETA for Canada) even for single-country visits. Plan accordingly if attending matches in multiple countries.

**Q: What's the best way to experience multiple matches?**
A: Purchase a team-following package or create your own itinerary focusing on one geographic region (e.g., East Coast USA cities, Mexico venues, or California stadiums) to minimize travel.

**Q: Are there payment plans for World Cup tickets?**
A: FIFA typically does not offer payment plans for individual tickets, but some hospitality packages through authorized sellers may offer installment options.

**Q: Can I resell my World Cup tickets?**
A: FIFA operates an official ticket resale platform. Selling tickets outside official channels may result in cancellation and can be risky. Always use authorized platforms.
